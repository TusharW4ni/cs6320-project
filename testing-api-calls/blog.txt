<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-how-to-make-vegan-mushroom-soup">How to make vegan mushroom soup</h2>



<p>Start by cooking the sliced mushrooms and onions in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté until they’re soft and fragrant.</p>



<p>Stir in the vegan Worcestershire sauce, then the carrots and garlic.</p>



<p>Add the dried sage, smoked paprika, wild rice blend, and vegetable broth to the pot. Stir to mix, then bring the soup up to a boil. Lower the heat and allow the soup to simmer, covered, until the rice is tender.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-frequently-asked-questions">Frequently asked questions</h2>



<div class="schema-faq wp-block-yoast-faq-block"><div class="schema-faq-section" id="faq-question-1696976684180"><strong class="schema-faq-question"><strong>Is wild rice actually rice?</strong></strong> <p class="schema-faq-answer">Fun fact: wild rice is a type of aquatic grass and isn’t related to <a href="https://www.noracooks.com/basmati-rice/">white</a> or brown rice at all. It most commonly grows in lakes and streams near the Great Lakes region of North America.<br/><br/>Despite its differences, wild rice is sold right next to regular rice in major grocery stores and cooks just the same, too. When it’s done, it has an almost nutty flavor and a chewy texture that compliments comforting meals like this vegan mushroom soup so well!</p> </div> <div class="schema-faq-section" id="faq-question-1696976685950"><strong class="schema-faq-question"><strong>How can I make this nut free?</strong></strong> <p class="schema-faq-answer">Simply swap the cashews for 1 cup of full fat coconut milk instead. Just stir it into the soup once the rice is cooked. The soup won&#8217;t be as thick, but it&#8217;s a great option and it makes the process easier (you don&#8217;t need a blender).</p> </div> <div class="schema-faq-section" id="faq-question-1696976687355"><strong class="schema-faq-question"><strong>What kind of mushrooms are best for vegan mushroom soup?</strong></strong> <p class="schema-faq-answer">Any kind, really. I like cremini mushrooms because they’re easy to find and have a mild flavor. To give the soup more dynamic flavors, use a blend of shiitake, oyster, baby bella, or portobello mushrooms instead.</p> </div> <div class="schema-faq-section" id="faq-question-1696976687998"><strong class="schema-faq-question"><strong>I don’t have wild rice. What can I use instead?</strong></strong> <p class="schema-faq-answer">You can swap the wild rice blend for white or brown rice, quinoa, barley, farro, or another grain you have on hand. Green or brown lentils might work as well. Just keep in mind that the cooking time will change depending on the grain/legume you use.</p> </div> <div class="schema-faq-section" id="faq-question-1696976688652"><strong class="schema-faq-question"><strong>How do you store the leftovers?</strong></strong> <p class="schema-faq-answer">Once the soup is cool, transfer it to an airtight container and store it in the fridge for 4 to 5 days. It also freezes well for about 3 months.</p> </div> </div>
